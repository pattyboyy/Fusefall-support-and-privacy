Fusefall

Privacy Policy

Effective date: March 7, 2026

This Privacy Policy explains what information Fusefall collects, how it is used, and how gameplay, purchases, and leaderboard features are supported on iPhone.

Information Fusefall Uses

  • Account information: when you sign in with Apple, Fusefall may store your Apple-provided account identifier, display name, and email address if Apple makes them available during sign-in.
  • Public leaderboard information: Fusefall stores the public leaderboard name you choose, your scores, your region code, and the time a leaderboard entry was created.
  • Purchase information: Fusefall uses Apple StoreKit transaction information to apply purchased credits and avoid duplicate crediting of the same transaction.
  • Gameplay and progression data: Fusefall stores local progress such as credits, unlocked skins, equipped skins, tutorial completion, and claimed challenge rewards on your device.

How Information Is Used

  • To sign you into the app and maintain a player profile.
  • To display your chosen public name on global and regional leaderboards.
  • To process and apply Apple in-app purchases for credits.
  • To save your local progress, cosmetics, and challenge rewards.
  • To detect whether a purchase transaction has already been credited.

Third-Party Services

Fusefall relies on the following third-party services to provide core app features:

  • Apple: Sign in with Apple and Apple In-App Purchases are provided through Apple system frameworks and services.
  • Firebase Realtime Database: leaderboard entries are sent to a Firebase Realtime Database so global and regional scores can be displayed across users.

Storage and Retention

Local game progress is stored on your device. Leaderboard entries submitted to the live leaderboard may remain in the leaderboard database after submission so rankings can be displayed to other players.

Data Sharing

Fusefall does not sell personal information. Information is shared only as needed to operate account sign-in, leaderboard functionality, and Apple in-app purchases.

Your Choices

  • You can choose your public leaderboard name during account setup.
  • You may stop using the app at any time.
  • Deleting the app from your device removes local device-stored game data, unless that data is restored from a later reinstall or sign-in flow.
